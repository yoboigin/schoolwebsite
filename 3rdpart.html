Insights into Climate Distress and Biodiversity Threats.

Drought

According to World Health Organization (WHO), Drought is a prolonged dry period in the natural climate cycle that can occur anywhere in the world. It is a slow-onset disaster characterized by the lack of precipitation, resulting in a water shortage. Droughts are the biggest threat to crops and livestock worldwide, affecting an estimated 55 million people annually in almost every region of the world. Drought exacerbates mass migration, puts people's livelihoods in jeopardy, and raises the risk of illness and death. Thirty million people could be displaced by 2030 due to drought, and forty percent of the world's population already suffers from water scarcity.

climate change

The environment, ecosystems, and human societies are all greatly impacted by the effects of climate change. The rise in the extreme weather events is one of the main effects. Increased frequency of heatwaves, droughts, floods, and hurricanes has severely damaged infrastructure, agriculture, and communities.

Pollution

Pollution is the introduction of harmful materials into the environment. These harmful materials are called pollutants. Pollutants can be natural, such as volcanic ash. They can also be created by human activity, such as trash or runoff produced by factories. Pollutants damage the quality of air, water, and land.

resource depletion

This occurs when the renewable and non-renewable natural resources become scarce because they are consumed faster than they can recover. The term resource depletion is commonly associated with water usage, fossil fuel consumption, trees and fishing. All of these resources have been depleted primarily because of human activities.

health risk

Environmental pollutants can cause health problems like respiratory diseases, heart disease, and some types of cancer People with low incomes are more likely to live in polluted areas and have unsafe drinking water. And children and pregnant women are at higher risk of health problems related to pollution.
